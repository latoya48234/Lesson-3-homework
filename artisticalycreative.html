Fading Desire


Alone, cold and empty underneath the stars,
I lay waiting, wanting, and hoping.
As the moon creeps in, a fire starts to burn within me,
No sign of rescue in sight.
Trapped amongst the flames, I struggle, trying to snuff the raging beast, The beast continues to stomp out my dreams, now dying embers.
The light that once shined bright starts to fade; to shrivel like a dead flower
Lying unwanted, like discarded trash.
Waiting with the friend of loneliness for a savior that will never show,
I lay dying for an eternity six feet below.

By Latoya Armstrong

I Want To Live

He watched her from behind the bushes in the park. She was lying in the grass out in the open staring at the twilight sky.
The park was located in the center of city, so there were a lot of people around. He would have to wait to make his move.
After fifteen minutes of waiting, she finally got up heading to her car. He followed her keeping his distance by hiding in
the shadows of the trees.

Outside of the park, he hid in the doorway of an abandon warehouse. She walked down the city street up to a black SUV parked
nearby. He wondered why she would park in such a secluded area. The street was lined with buildings casting dark shadows over
the street. He was grateful for her choice in location. It would make it easier for him.

She approached the SUV on the back passenger side. After opening the door, she began searching for something. Now was the moment
for him to do it. No was around or looking. “She’s waiting on you. Go now!” he told himself. Stealthy he walked up behind her
hitting her at the base of her skull knocking her out. She fell halfway into the back of the car. He eased her in the rest of the
way taking the keys out of her pocket. “That was easier than I thought it would be.” he said to himself as he climbed into the driver
seat. It had been twenty years since he’d seen her. To him, she still looked the same...by Latoya Armstrong
